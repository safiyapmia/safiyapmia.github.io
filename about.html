Hi, I am Safiya Palesa Mia.

I am a Mechatronic Engineering student from South Africa.

My name tells my story: Safiya comes from my faith, Islam; Palesa is Sotho, from the country where I was born and raised; and Mia is my Indian family name. Together they remind me of the beauty of diversity and why equality matters.

I chose engineering because I’ve always enjoyed making and fixing things, from small hacks at home to more ambitious projects. I’m curious, I like solving problems, and I want to use that to create technology that is practical and useful—especially here in Africa.

I believe good engineering is not just about neat wiring, clean code, and working systems—it’s also about people. For me, teamwork, teaching, and designing inclusively are as important as the technical details.

My goal is simple: to raise the standard of living through mechatronics that is affordable, maintainable, and actually gets used.
