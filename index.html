Hi, I’m Safiya Palesa Mia.

My name is a map of my home: an Arabic first name, a Sotho second name, and an Indian surname. I’m South African, proudly mixed—influenced by many worlds and comfortable building bridges between them. That shows up in my engineering too.

I chose engineering because solving physical and mathematical problems feels like play. I’m fuelled by curiosity and a stubborn desire to make useful things—especially for Africa. I’m drawn to projects where hardware meets software and people, and where craft and compassion both matter.

Personal statement

Why engineering: From a young age I loved making things with my hands—fixes at home, scarecrows for the veggie patch, little hacks that made life easier. I admired Nikola Tesla not just for invention, but for the idea that technology should widen access. Learning about child labour and cycles of exploitation sharpened that belief. I want robots and tools to take on the dangerous, dirty work—and return dignity and time to people.

How I work: iterate fast, document clearly, and make the inside as neat as the outside (wiring, plots, code comments). I care about teaming and teaching; good engineering is communal.

What I’m aiming for: to raise the standard of living—starting here in Africa—through mechatronics that is affordable, maintainable, and actually gets used.
